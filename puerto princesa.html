Puerto Princesa

Puerto Princesa Travel Requirements & Guidelines | COVID-19 New Normal

1. Who are allowed to go to Puerto Princesa?

Puerto Princesa in Palawan is open to tourists regardless of vaccination status, provided they meet all the requirements. Fully vaccinated individuals, regardless of their LGU of origin, are exempted from presenting a negative COVID-19 test result and just need to present proof of their vaccination status, like a vaccination card or certificate. Unvaccinated or partially vaccinated travelers coming from areas under Alert Level 1 are also exempted from the testing requirement, but those coming from areas under Alert Level 2 or higher must show a negative antigen test taken within 24 hours before their trip.

2. What are the entry points in Puerto Princesa?

The main entry point in Puerto Princesa is Puerto Princesa International Airport.

3. What travel requirements do you need to prepare?

Here are the requirements that you need to prepare before going to Puerto Princesa:

Prepare your Vaccination Card or Certificate (For fully vaccinated)

As proof of your vaccination status, you need to show a vaccination card or certificate.

Secure a Negative COVID-19 Test Result (For Unvaccinated and Partially Vaccinated coming from Alert Level 2 and higher)

Unvaccinated and partially vaccinated travelers coming from areas under Alert Level 2 or higher need to present a negative antigen test taken within 24 hours prior to their travel.

4. What are the guidelines and protocols to follow when you're in Puerto Princesa?

Once you're in Puerto Princesa, make sure you follow these safety protocols:

Minimum health standards

Domestic tourists must utilize the proper use of face masks, wash hands regularly, and practice social distancing, especially in public. This is to stop the spread of COVID-19.
